The Indie Behind the Games

I'm Alex S Johnson, an indie game developer who believes the best games come from raw experimentation and unfiltered creativity. I started making games in a cramped apartment with nothing but a laptop and an obsession with quirky mechanics that mainstream studios wouldn't touch.

What fascinates me? Games that break conventions, embrace imperfection, and tell stories through gameplay rather than cutscenes. I'm drawn to systems that surprise even me as the creator — emergent chaos that feels alive. My philosophy is simple: ship experiments, learn fast, and never polish away the personality.

Every project on this site represents a piece of that journey — from half-baked prototypes to fully realized worlds. Some are polished, some are rough, but all of them are honest expressions of what indie game development means to me.

How the jank got here

Every bug, every weird mechanic, every "wait, that actually works?" moment—here's the messy, honest journey of building games the indie way.

First Prototype

Started with a janky physics experiment that barely ran at 20fps. The controls were backwards, the camera spun wildly, but something about the chaos felt right. That broken prototype became the foundation.

First Public Release

Threw it on itch.io with a "this is weird but try it" description. Woke up to 200 downloads and comments like "I don't understand this but I can't stop playing." That's when I knew the jank was a feature, not a bug.

The Mechanic Breakthrough

Accidentally inverted a collision check while fixing a crash. Suddenly players could phase through walls if they jumped at the right angle. Instead of fixing it, I built an entire level around it. Best design decision I never meant to make.

Community Moment

Someone made a speedrun video exploiting every broken mechanic I thought was embarrassing. The community rallied around it. I learned that "unpolished" doesn't mean "unloved"—it means authentic.

Current Direction

Working on the next project with the same philosophy: embrace the weird, trust the chaos, and never sand off the rough edges that make it feel human. The jank continues, and I wouldn't have it any other way.

How I build

I iterate through rapid playtesting cycles, constantly refining mechanics based on real player feedback. I embrace rough edges—they're part of the charm—and only polish what truly enhances the experience without losing personality.

Prototype fast

Get the core mechanic playable within hours, not days. Ugly placeholders and programmer art are perfectly fine—what matters is testing the fun factor immediately.

Playtest honestly

Watch real players struggle, laugh, and rage-quit. Their unfiltered reactions reveal what's broken and what's brilliant—no sugarcoating allowed.

Polish selectively

Only refine what enhances the personality—smooth out game-breaking bugs, but keep the quirky animations and weird physics that make it uniquely mine.

What I won't compromise on

These aren't just principles—they're the foundation of every project I build. In an industry obsessed with polish and profit, I choose creativity, honesty, and the freedom to make something genuinely weird.

Player curiosity

Games should spark questions, not just provide answers. I design systems that invite experimentation and reward exploration, even if it means breaking the "rules" of conventional game design.

Honest production

No fake promises, no manufactured hype. What you see in development is what you get in the final game. I share the messy process, the failures, and the unexpected wins—because that's the real story of indie game development.

Humor without cynicism

Comedy doesn't have to be mean-spirited. I aim for laughs that come from absurdity, surprise, and genuine charm—not at the expense of players or the medium itself.

Freedom to be weird

The best games embrace their quirks instead of sanding them down. I protect the rough edges, the unexpected mechanics, and the unconventional ideas that make a project uniquely mine.
