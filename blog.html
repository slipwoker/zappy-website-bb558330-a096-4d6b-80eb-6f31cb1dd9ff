Dev Journal: Unfiltered & In-Progress

Welcome to the raw, honest feed from the trenches of indie game development. Here you'll find design opinions that might change tomorrow, small experiments that sometimes work, and updates on projects as they evolve—not after they're polished. This is where ideas get tested in public and imperfect progress gets celebrated.

Expect candid thoughts on game mechanics, art direction pivots, technical hiccups, and the occasional existential crisis about whether that feature really needs to ship. If you're looking for glossy press releases, this isn't it. If you want to see how the sausage gets made (quirks and all), you're in the right place.

Posting cadence: Whenever there's something worth sharing—could be weekly during active dev sprints, could be radio silence during deep work phases. Scope includes game design thoughts, dev logs, tool experiments, and the occasional rant about why physics engines are both amazing and terrible. Shipping imperfectly and learning in public is the whole point.

Released my first public demo and within 20 minutes, players discovered you could clip through walls by jumping backwards while crouching. Budget Audio Design: Kitchen Utensils as Sound Effects

Can't afford professional sound design? Neither could I. My sword-fighting sounds came from hitting wooden spoons together, footsteps were recorded by walking on bubble wrap, and that epic explosion? A bag of chips crushed near a microphone. Indie game audio development gets weird, and I love it.

The 3 AM Breakthrough: Why Exhaustion Breeds Innovation

Something magical happens when you've been coding for 14 hours straight and your brain stops working normally. That's when I accidentally invented my game's signature mechanic – by mistyping a variable name and creating the weirdest physics glitch that somehow became the core gameplay loop. Sleep deprivation: not recommended, but occasionally brilliant.
