Quick answers

Common questions about working together, getting in touch, and supporting indie game development.

What types of collaboration are you open to?

I'm interested in guest posts, game jams, cross-promotion with fellow indie devs, and honest playtesting exchanges. I prefer projects that embrace the quirky, experimental side of game development rather than polished commercial ventures.

How do you prefer to receive feedback on your games?

Direct and honest is best. I appreciate constructive criticism that focuses on gameplay mechanics, bugs, and user experience. Feel free to be blunt—I value authenticity over sugar-coating. Bug reports with screenshots or videos are especially helpful.

Can I stream or create content about your games?

Absolutely! You have full permission to stream, record, review, or create any content featuring my games. No need to ask for permission—just credit the game and link back if possible. I love seeing how people react to the chaos.

What's your typical reply time for messages?

I aim to respond within 2-3 business days for most inquiries. During active development sprints or game jams, it might take a bit longer. If your message is time-sensitive, mention it in the subject line and I'll prioritize accordingly.

How can I support your indie game development?

The best support is playing the games, sharing them with friends, and providing honest feedback. If you want to go further, consider leaving reviews on itch.io or Steam, joining the community discussions, or supporting via Patreon when available. Word-of-mouth is gold for indie devs.
